Game Rules

Understanding the basics of football rules is essential for anyone looking to enjoy the game. Here, we cover the fundamental rules of football.

1. The Objective

The main objective of football is to score more goals than the opposing team. Each team tries to send the ball into the opposing teamâ€™s goal while preventing the opposing team from doing the same.

2. The Field

Football is played on a rectangular field with goals at each end. The field size varies, but it generally ranges from 100 to 130 yards in length and 50 to 100 yards in width.

3. Duration of the Game

A standard football match lasts 90 minutes, split into two 45-minute halves. If necessary, additional time may be added for stoppages.

4. Players and Positions

Each team consists of 11 players: a goalkeeper, defenders, midfielders, and forwards. Each position has specific roles, but all players can move freely on the field.

5. Scoring a Goal

A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the goalposts. The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.

6. Fouls and Penalties

Players must avoid fouls, such as tripping or pushing opponents. Severe fouls may lead to free kicks, penalty kicks, or even yellow or red cards.
